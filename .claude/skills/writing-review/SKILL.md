# Writing review

A document succeeds when the intended reader gets what they need in one pass. Review for the reader, not for the author.

## Pass 1 — Reader fit (the pass that matters most)

- **Who reads this, and what do they DO after reading?** If the document doesn't serve that action, no amount of polish saves it.
- **Lead with the payload.** Conclusion, recommendation, or answer first; background after. Readers who want the reasoning will keep reading; readers who don't shouldn't have to.
- **One document, one job.** Mixed audiences (exec summary + implementation detail) get explicit sections, not interleaving.
- Does the title/first line let someone decide whether to read it at all?

## Pass 2 — Substance

- Every claim: supported (source, number, example) or cut. "Significantly improves" with no number is decoration.
- Every number: recomputed or traced to source (verification skill).
- Hedge audit: keep hedges that convey real uncertainty ("unverified", "as of June"); cut hedges that convey cowardice ("somewhat", "arguably", "it could be said").
- What would a hostile reader attack first? Fix or preempt it.

## Pass 3 — Line editing

- Cut ruthlessly: if a sentence survives with a word removed, remove the word. Target: -20% length with zero meaning lost. Read it aloud mentally; stumbles mark rewrites.
- Kill throat-clearing openers ("It is important to note that...", "In today's world..."), empty intensifiers ("very", "extremely", "incredibly"), and unnecessary meta-commentary about the document itself.
- Concrete beats abstract: "cuts build time from 4 min to 40 s" > "dramatically improves performance".
- Parallel structure in lists; consistent terminology (one name per concept — synonyms are for novels).
- Formatting serves scanning: headers that summarize (not "Overview"), lists only where order/enumeration matters, bold only for genuine keystones.

## Mechanics gate (always, last)
Names spelled right (people, products, files), dates correct against today's date, links resolve, code blocks run, numbers consistent between summary and body, no leftover placeholders (TODO, TKTK, <name>, lorem).

## Verdict
Like the critique skill: findings with severity, then loop. A review that only fixes commas has failed — Pass 1 findings are the reason this skill exists.
